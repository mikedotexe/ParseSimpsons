"Bye Bye Nerdie" is the sixteenth episode of the twelfth season of The Simpsons. It aired on March 11, 2001. In the episode, when she becomes the target of a female bully, Lisa discovers a scientific reason as to why bullies pick on nerds. Meanwhile, Homer goes into the baby-proofing business, which is threatening the businesses of pediatricians and greeting card salesmen.

The episode is written by John Frink and Don Payne and directed Lauren MacMullan and features guest star Kathy Griffin.

[edit] Plot

When Marge finds out that the school bus is only 2 blocks away from their house, she goes temporarily insane, and injures Bart and Lisa while forcing them to get ready for school, and forces them to choke their food down. She then runs outside to find the bus has gone. Marge's insanity takes a turn for the worse when she tries to outrun the school bus. She succeeds, but Bart and Lisa tell her that they are right next to the school. Marge threatens to hurt them if they do not get on the bus. At school, Lisa attempts to make friends with a new girl, Francine, but Francine beats up Lisa severely. Even attempting to share an interest in Malibu Stacy does not work when Francine steals and ruins her Malibu Stacy doll. Lisa hires the school bullies (Nelson, Dolph, Jimbo, and Kearney) to protect her, but they refuse since girls fight dirtier than boys (and sometimes, they fall in love). It is up to Lisa to investigate the reason why Francine is beating her up.

Meanwhile, Homer, fearing Maggie could die from touching things unsafe to her when he hears this from a baby-proofing saleswoman, starts his own baby-proofing crusade, selling cheap but safe and effective products and making Springfield safe for children, but it causes the baby-injury-related industries in Springfield to be in decline.

Lisa does scientific research on nerds, and finds out that the odor of chemical nerd pheromone "poindextrose" attracts bullies like Francine, proving that both nerds and bullies are predisposed to be what they are. Lisa then tests the "poindextrose" extracted from the nerds on Drederick Tatum when he visits Springfield Elementary, which causes Nelson to start punching him uncontrollably and also gives him a wedgie. Lisa demonstrates her experiment at Professor Frink's science contest, the "12th Annual Big Science Thing", using the antidote on herself and making Francine peaceful and friendly towards her, the antidote being just salad dressing which covers up the smell of the poindextrose. At the end, the salad dressing runs out and Francine goes on a rampage beating up all the scientists in the room.

[edit] Production

The episode was by John Frink and Don Payne and directed by Lauren MacMullan. When it was pitched it was originally that Lisa punched Francine, but would miss and punch Principal Skinner and was sent to juvenile hall and they make illegal blue jeans.[1] The subplot of baby safing was based on when a salesman came in and showed how a baby could be killed somehow.[1] The joke where the salesman fails at opening the baby formula was later used as a test on a new writer.[2] Lauren designed Francine to be a professional wrestler and look like a business man.[3] During the recording sessions the staff expected Kathy Griffin, who voices Francine, to talk a lot as she is known for it, but did not talk as much as they expected.[4]

[edit] Cultural references

The episode also features NRBQ's song "Always Safety First"[2], played during a montage in which Homer systematically improves the safety of the infants of Springfield.

When Kent Brockman announces that a safe-baby craze is sweeping Springfield, Homer celebrates by performing his interpretation of The Safety Dance.

C. Everett Koop is seen in the audience near the end of the episode.[6]

[edit] Reception

In its original American broadcast, "Bye Bye Nerdie" was viewed by 8.8914 million viewers with a 8.7 rating/14 share ranking 26 in the weekly ratings making it the top animated show of the week.[7]

Judge Mac McEntire of DVD Verdict said the greatest moment of the episode was the kids imagining what a new kid in school might be like.[8] Colin Jacobson of DVD Movie Guide gave the episode a positive review saying "While this isn’t saying much, “Nerdie” provides one of Season 12’s better shows. Both plots work well, though I prefer the childproofing side of things; it peters out at the end, but it has some good bits. The episode keeps us interested and entertained"[9]
