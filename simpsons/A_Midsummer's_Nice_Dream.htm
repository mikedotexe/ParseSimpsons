"A Midsummer's Nice Dream" is the sixteenth episode of The Simpsons' twenty-second season. It aired on the Fox network in the United States on March 13, 2011. The episode name is a play on the Shakespeare play A Midsummer Night's Dream and the Cheech & Chong movie Nice Dreams.

[edit] Plot

After learning of an upcoming visit by Cheech & Chong, the Simpsons visit a local theater to view their live stage act. While performing, Chong, becoming upset by the repetitiveness of the act, begins to improvise, ultimately leaving the stage. After prompting, Homer takes the place of Chong and begins to recite the lines of the act from memory. Cheech is impressed, and asks Homer to join him on tour in place of Chong. The duo title their act Cheech and Chunk. Homer is disillusioned when he discovers that Cheech and Chong's lives are different from the personas depicted. Later, Chong begins auditioning a replacement for Cheech. He ultimately chooses Seymour Skinner, titling their act Teech and Chong; the team proves unsuccessful. Later, Homer convinces Chong to rejoin Cheech on stage to perform. Bart gives an epilogue to the story and tells the audience to watch them tomorrow on Hulu.com.

Meanwhile, Marge discovers that the Crazy Cat Lady is a hoarder. In an effort to help, Marge has the clutter removed from her home. After loading the waste disposal truck, Marge begins removing items, causing her own home to become cluttered. Eventually, Homer brings back the Crazy Cat Lady to change Marge's new ways, but ends up becoming a hoarder again after seeing all her old items. Homer and Marge eventually just leave her in their house and go off to reunite Cheech and Chong, as the Crazy Cat Lady starts to shout and bang her chest like Tarzan, drawing all of her cats into the house.

[edit] Reception

In its original American broadcast, "A Midsummer's Nice Dream" was viewed by an estimated 5.448 million households and received a 2.5 rating/8% share among adults between the ages of 18 and 49.[2] This means that it was seen by 2.5% of all 18–49 year olds, and 8% of all 18–49 year olds watching television at the time of the broadcast. This episode fell 11% in the ratings from the previous episode, making it the lowest rated episode of the season.[2][3]

The Parents Television Council named "A Midsummer's Nice Dream" its Worst TV Show of the Week ending the week on March 18, 2011 for its subject plot, marking it as the first time an episode of The Simpsons has been named the Worst TV Show of the Week by the PTC. The PTC stated "Remember when The Simpsons was the most controversial show on television? Ah, those were the days. Long before Seth MacFarlane hijacked Sunday nights on Fox with his clunky set-ups, forced irreverence, and puerile attempts at satire, The Simpsons' portrayal of the modern American family drew plenty of ire from media critics who questioned whether Bart Simpson should really be a role model for young viewers," and concluded with "The Simpsons may have been on the air for 22 years now (and counting), and while there are plenty of adults who watch the show, no one can deny that there is also a sizable audience of children. Since Cheech is a portrayed as a straight-laced stick-in-the-mud, the subtle anti-drug messages in the show are completely lost. In the end, sober still equals boring."[4]

[edit] Opening sequence

Lisa plays the trumpet instead of the saxophone in the opening scene, but still has the saxophone in her bike basket on her way home. During the episode Lots-O'-Huggin' Bear from Toy Story 3 makes an appearance when showing The Crazy Cat Lady's hoarded items.
