"Guess Who's Coming to Criticize Dinner?" is the third episode of the 11th season of The Simpsons. It originally aired on October 24, 1999.[1] Ed Asner guest stars as the newspaper editor.[1]

[edit] Plot

 This article's plot summary may be too long or excessively detailed. Please help improve it by removing unnecessary details and making it more concise. (September 2011)

Springfield Elementary School arranges for the students to tour the Springfield Shopper newspaper office, while the original intent is for the faculty to throw a party in their absence. Homer skips work (leaving behind a terribly constructed dummy and a tape of himself singing to which Mr. Burns actually orders promotion to a higher position at which point the tape player malfunctions and sets fire to the office) to serve as a chaperone for the field trip, but en route he shouts obscenities at an ambulance and inadvertently drives Bart, Lisa, Milhouse, and Nelson to the zoo by mistake before arriving at the newspaper office. They arrive just in time for the tour, which passes a characteristically desperate Gil among other Shopper telemarketers trying to sell newspaper subscriptions. The tour ventures through the inner workings of the newspaper, including passing the cartoonist behind Mary Worth (whom none of the students actually read) and Dear Abby and Ann Landers who are held captive for 23 hours a day in hyperbaric chambers. As Lisa is upset to hear that the Shopper does not contain any recycled material, Homer soon gets distracted by smelling cake from a distance and wanders into a room where newspaper staff are celebrating the retirement of their cynical food critic. When questioned why he is eating everything at the party, he expresses his love for all foods in a short song, and the editor decides to give Homer a tryout, and that he will fill the now vacant position if it is deemed good enough.

When Homer gets home, he tries to type an example food review, but the E on their typewriter does not work – making it almost impossible for Homer to type anything since E is the most used letter in the English language. The editor of the newspaper reads Homer's review and proclaims it to be a joke since Homer's review contains gross misspellings, numerous threatening references to the United Nations, and is completed by repeating the words "SCREW FLANDERS" over and over again in order to break his 500-word limit (Although this would be impossible because the typewriter does not have an operational E, unless Homer misspelled "Screw Flanders"). When the editor declares disgust with Homer's review, Homer pretends that it is really a joke, and runs off claiming he will be back with the real review. Upset (crying in the shower trying to scrub off his failure), Lisa offers to help Homer, who is absolutely terrible at trying to write a review. She fluffs it up with impressive, obscure words in a review Homer relates to her of a meal he had eaten when the family went out to dinner the previous night, and the editor is now impressed and gives Homer the job.

Homer then travels to various restaurants to review, but Homer's love for food causes all of his reviews to be exceptionally high (including one rating of nine thumbs up), while Lisa continues to be the unpaid ghostwriter to all of his reviews. Homer's constantly impressive reviews prompt praise from other townspeople (Lenny declares that "I never knew everything was so good!" and Mr. Burns becomes both jolly and so fat that his shinbones fracture), and conversely criticism from the other Shopper critics, who contend that he must be meaner instead of giving everything overwhelmingly good reviews. Homer then turns into a prude critic and negatively dictates his reviews of every restaurant he eats at for no apparent reason, which attracts the anger of restaurant owners who believe his reviews are bad for business. He even gives Marge's dinner the lowest review he had dished out since he was hired at the newspaper (seven thumbs up), and Lisa refuses to help Homer out anymore. Erecting a brave façade to flaunt his supposed power, Homer claims that he will never get his comeuppance as the most powerful food critic in Springfield; but in desperation, he turns to Maggie and Santa's Little Helper to help him write his next article. Due to their terribly limited vocabulary, all they can offer are words such as “sucks”, “chewy”, “bark”, “ruff”, and [angry growls]. The editor calls Homer's review garbage and even asks if it was written by a dog.

Meanwhile, all of the restaurant owners meet to try to get rid of Homer, and when the proposal to bar Homer from all Springfield restaurants is voted down, Akira suggests that they simply murder Homer at the upcoming Taste of Springfield Festival that will feature small booths containing samples from every restaurant for Homer and others to review. They all agree, and a French chef proclaims that he will kill Homer with a fantastically unhealthy éclair – one that contains over one million calories, 25 pounds of butter per square inch, and is slathered with chocolate so dark that light cannot escape. To deflect the possibility that Homer will survive the ordeal and the doubts of his colleagues, he says he is going to fill it with poison as well, and they all have a good laugh at Homer's impending "death by chocolate".

At the festival, against Marge's cautionary remarks, Homer gorges himself at every restaurant booth, unaware that the owners are out to get him. He eats at every booth and summarily gives them all bad reviews. Bart overhears a conversation between Akira and the Sea Captain discussing the planned murder of Homer. He alerts the family, who then tries to find Homer before it is too late. Meanwhile, Homer is incredibly full from eating so much, and he eventually stumbles upon the Frenchman's booth, where he has the deadly éclair ready for Homer. Lisa rushes in just in time to stop Homer by yelling "It's low fat!". In shocked disgust, he tosses the éclair behind him, causing it to explode on Hans Moleman's stand. The police rush to the scene and arrest the Frenchman, but he escapes after they start talking about what kind of breakfast they want to go eat. Homer and Lisa walk off into the distance with an angry mob stalking him. After the scene goes black and the end credits begin, Homer is clearly heard receiving his comeuppance, in the form of a beating, from the restaurant owners.

[edit] Cultural references

The song Homer sings upon being given the food critic job is set to the tune of "I Feel Pretty" from the musical West Side Story.[1] The Newspaper Editor (voiced by Edward Asner) is based on Lou Grant, the character Asner played in the series The Mary Tyler Moore Show and Lou Grant.[1] The restaurant Planet Springfield is a parody of Planet Hollywood, containing items such as the film script for The Cable Guy (1996), Herbie from The Love Bug (1968), a model of the RMS Titanic from Titanic (1997), an alien similar to those from Mars Attacks! (1996), models of a TIE Fighter, an X-Wing and C-3PO from Star Wars, as well as "the coffee mug" from the film Heartbeeps (1981) and "the cane" from Citizen Kane (1941), which is not a real prop.[1]
