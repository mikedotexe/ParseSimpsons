"Donnie Fatso" is the ninth episode of The Simpsons' twenty-second season. It aired on the Fox Network in the United States on December 12, 2010.[2] It was directed by Ralph Sosa and written by Chris Cluess. The plot revolves around an FBI agent helping Homer go undercover to infiltrate Fat Tony's mob. Homer agrees to this in hopes of decreasing his sentence after being charged for bribery. Jon Hamm guest starred as an FBI agent and Joe Mantegna reprises his role as Fat Tony and also plays his cousin, Fit Tony.

[edit] Plot

Homer and Marge wake on New Year's Day with hangovers after the family's New Year's Eve celebration. Homer takes out the garbage but Chief Wiggum and his cops arrive and inform Homer that the town has approved new dumb laws and Homer racks up a series of spurious fines. With fines of $1000, Moe suggests Homer bribe an official, but Wiggum catches him in the act and arrests him. After being sentenced to ten years in jail, Wiggum takes pity on Homer and suggests he talk with an FBI investigator who offers to reduce his jail time if Homer agrees to go undercover as an informant to investigate Fat Tony. Meanwhile, Marge becomes upset by Homer's "disappearance".

After freeing Homer from jail, Fat Tony lets Homer spend time with his gang. Impressed with Homer's attitude, Fat Tony decides to test him. His first order was to burn down Moe's Tavern (a revenge for the latter's rudeness towards Tony when he mistakes Tony's telephone call for a prank), but fortunately, Homer does not need to lift a finger as Moe already has done this in order to avoid sanitary inspection ("it's that or put in a ladies room!"). Homer is accepted into the mafia family and forges a special bond with Fat Tony. However, internal conflict sets in between his obligation to the government and his loyalty to his new 'family'. Fat Tony soon realizes that Homer is an informant and, emotionally unable to endure the betrayal, dies of heart attack.

Marge is pleased that Homer has his sentence scrapped and is able to come back home. Unable to bear the guilt from being associated with the death of Fat Tony, Homer decides to apologise to him at his grave, only to have Fat Tony's cousin Fit Tony kidnap him to settle a score. However, after Homer explains that he does not care about death, they have a conversation and come to the conclusion that the real culprits for killing Fat Tony were the government agents who spied on them. Fit Tony then assumes the family's management, but the stress of his new position makes him eat much more than the normal and he ends up becoming the new Fat Tony (another meta referential joke about the Simpsons' status quo).

[edit] Production

Jon Hamm, best known for playing Don Draper on Mad Men, guest starred in the episode as the FBI agent.[3] Hamm said that appearing in the show was "an incredible experience, I got to work on The Simpsons, which I watched for 20 years and the show is still fresh and still funny and the characters still resonate. It’s one of the best shows on television. It was an honor to be asked to be a part of it."[4] Joe Mantegna returned as Fat Tony, and voiced Fat Tony's cousin Fit Tony.[2]

[edit] Cultural references

The episode's plot and title are a parody of the film, Donnie Brasco.[5] The plot also makes references to the film, Goodfellas.[6] For example, Homer's monologue at the end of the episode serves as a homage to Henry Hill's monologue at the end of Goodfellas.[6] As with the monologue in that film, Homer's monologue is set to Sid Vicious' version of "My Way". There is also a reference to Casino, with Fit Tony narrowly avoiding a car bomb. Fat Tony's final words to Homer are "I loved you, man", which is a reference to the TV show Wiseguy's first story arc. The mobster Sonny Steelgrave says the same thing to Vinnie Terranova, who, like Homer with Fat Tony, has betrayed him. The opening sequence featured a Fox News helicopter with the words "Merry Christmas from Fox News… But no other holidays."[7]

[edit] Reception

In its original American broadcast on December 12, 2010, "Donnie Fatso" was viewed by an estimated 7.31 million viewers and received a 3.2 rating/8% share among adults between the ages of 18 and 49 coming third in its timeslot.[8][9] The episode also became the second highest rated program on Fox Broadcasting Company that night after Family Guy.[8] The episode also became the third highest rated show on the network the week it originally aired after Glee and Family Guy.[10]

Todd VanDerWerff gave the episode a mediocre review, calling it "Fairly lazy", writing that "There were things I laughed at here [...], but the storyline was too much of a mob movie pastiche, taken as it was from equal parts Goodfellas and Donnie Brasco.".[6] He ultimately gave the episode a C, the lowest rating of "Animation Domination" after Family Guy and American Dad.[6] Entertainment Weekly writer Darren Franich named Fat Tony's death the 9th Best TV Character Death of 2010 writing that "give the writers some credit for doing something different: In the middle of an otherwise straightforward mob-themed episode, they actually went ahead and killed off Fat Tony, a character who was first introduced almost twenty years ago."[11] He later went on to criticize the addition of Fit Tony.[11]

Dan Castellaneta's performance in the episode, as Homer, Barney, Krusty and Louie, was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Voice-Over Performance at the 63rd Primetime Emmy Awards.[12]
